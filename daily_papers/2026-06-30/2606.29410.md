# Flow-polarity decoupling and universal mobility enhancement in dense bacterial active fluids with mesoscale order

**arXiv**: [2606.29410](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.29410)
**Authors**: Yuhao Wang, Premkumar Leishangthem, Yiming Ding, Xinliang Xu, Yilin Wu
**Categories**: cond-mat.soft

## Abstract

arXiv:2606.29410v1 Announce Type: cross 
Abstract: Active fluids consisting of living cells or synthetic microswimmers display rich emergent behavior and nonequilibrium mechanical properties, which not only shed light on various biological processes but also inform the engineering of autonomous fluidics and self-driven materials. The individual behavior of microswimmers and their interaction with self-generated mesoscale solvent flows underlie the emergent properties of active fluids. Here we studied the microscopic dynamics in dense 3D bacterial active fluids by simultaneous imaging of cell body, flagella, and flow field. A surprising finding is that the polarity of cells was randomly distributed in mesoscale flow regimes, and yet the system displays mesoscale order in the self-generated solvent flows. Despite the apparent flow-polarity decoupling, the motion of cells relative to local solvent flows predominantly navigated upstream, with the self-advection speed universally enhanced by a flow-controlled constant. Numerical modeling with full hydrodynamic interactions reveals that the observed flow-polarity decoupling arises from the breakdown of the commonly held force-dipole assumption for anisotropic microswimmers: in the presence of flow gradient and near-field hydrodynamic interactions, the direction of total active forcing exerted by a swimming bacterium to the surrounding fluid no longer aligns with its polarity. The simulations suggest that near-field interactions serve as a new type of emergent, configuration-dependent active forcing, which profoundly impact self-organization and transport in dense bacterial suspensions. Taken together, our work establishes fundamental knowledge for faithfully understanding the collective behavior of dense polar active fluids.

# Flow-polarity decoupling and universal mobility enhancement in dense bacterial active fluids with mesoscale order

> **ArXiv**: [2606.29410](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.29410) | **Authors**: Yuhao Wang, Premkumar Leishangthem, Yiming Ding, Xinliang Xu, Yilin Wu

# 论文快速阅读笔记

## 一句话总结
本文通过实验和模拟发现，在密集的3D细菌活性流体中，细菌的极性方向与介观尺度溶剂流场之间存在解耦现象，并揭示了上游定向运动导致的普遍迁移率增强机制。

## 研究问题
密集细菌活性流体中，微观游泳者的个体行为与自生介观溶剂流场之间的相互作用如何影响系统的自组织与输运性质？特别是，细菌的极性（方向）与流场之间是否存在直接的耦合关系？

## 核心方法
- 同时成像细菌的细胞体、鞭毛和流场，实现微观动力学测量。
- 结合全流体动力学相互作用的数值模拟，分析活性力施加方向与极性的关系。

## 主要结果
- 细菌极性在介观流区中随机分布，但自生溶剂流场却呈现出介观尺度的有序性（流-极性解耦）。
- 细菌相对局部流场的运动呈现上游导航趋势，其自迁移动速度被一个流控常数普遍增强。
- 模拟揭示解耦源于对非对称微游泳者力偶极子假设的失效：在流梯度与近场流体动力学相互作用下，细菌施加的活性总力方向不再与其极性对齐。
- 近场相互作用构成一种新的依赖于构型的活性力，深刻影响致密悬浮液的自组织与输运。

## 潜在价值
- 修正了传统活性流体理论中对微生物施加力偶极子方向的假设，为理解密集极性活性流体的集体行为建立了基础性知识。
- 对工程化自驱动材料、自主流体系统以及生物过程（如生物膜形成、细菌集群）的建模具有指导意义。
