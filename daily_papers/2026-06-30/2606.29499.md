# Emergence of beating in a magnetic flagellum consisting of active bots

**arXiv**: [2606.29499](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.29499)
**Authors**: Francisca Guzm\'an-Lastra, Daniel Hern\'andez, Nicol\'as Quintriqueo, Enkeleida Lushi, Erick Burgos
**Categories**: cond-mat.soft

## Abstract

arXiv:2606.29499v1 Announce Type: new 
Abstract: We investigate the emergence of flagellar beating in chains of magnetic self--propelled particles (MSPPs) built from centimeter--scale vibrating robots (Hexbugs) with embedded neodymium dipoles. When one end of the chain is anchored and self--propulsion is activated, longitudinal stress accumulates along the chain until it overcomes the magnetic bending stiffness, triggering a buckling instability that drives sustained flagellar beating. Using a combination of experiments and numerical simulations, we identify three distinct dynamical regimes straight chain, stable flagellar beating, and fission governed by the competition between active force, chain length, and magnetic bending stiffness. The onset of beating requires a seed misalignment set by the balance between magnetic torques and rotational noise, and we show that the transition corresponds to a supercritical Hopf bifurcation. A kinematic model reproduces the observed orientation dynamics with excellent agreement. The magnetic bending stiffness, which arises directly from dipole--dipole interactions, is fully tunable via dipole strength and chain length, offering independent experimental control over both activity and rigidity. Our results establish a macroscopic platform for studying force-induced buckling and self--oscillations in active filaments, with direct connections to flagellar motion in biological and synthetic microswimmers.

# Emergence of beating in a magnetic flagellum consisting of active bots

> **ArXiv**: [2606.29499](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.29499) | **Authors**: Francisca Guzm\'an-Lastra, Daniel Hern\'andez, Nicol\'as Quintriqueo, Enkeleida Lushi, Erick Burgos

# 论文阅读笔记

## 一句话总结
本文通过实验和模拟，展示了由磁性自推进粒子（MSPP）组成的链在固定一端时，因纵向应力克服磁弯曲刚度而触发屈曲不稳定性，从而产生持续鞭毛式拍打运动。

## 研究问题
如何利用宏观活性粒子链模拟生物鞭毛的拍打运动？其动态行为（如拍打、断裂）如何受活性力、链长和磁弯曲刚度的调控？

## 核心方法
- **实验系统**：使用厘米级振动机器人（Hexbugs）嵌入钕磁偶极子，构建磁自推进粒子链。
- **数值模拟**：结合实验观测，分析三种动力学状态（直链、稳定拍打、断裂）。
- **理论分析**：通过运动学模型重现方向动力学，并指出拍打触发对应超临界Hopf分岔，需要种子失准（由磁扭矩与旋转噪声平衡产生）。

## 主要结果
- 识别出三种动态状态：直链、稳定鞭毛拍打、链断裂，并由活性力、链长和磁弯曲刚度之间的竞争决定。
- 拍打起始需要种子失准，该失准由磁扭矩与旋转噪声平衡产生，且转变对应于超临界Hopf分岔。
- 运动学模型与实验观测高度吻合。
- 磁弯曲刚度可通过偶极强度和链长完全调节，从而独立控制活性和刚度。

## 潜在价值
为研究活性细丝中的力诱导屈曲和自激振荡提供了宏观实验平台，直接关联生物和合成微游动体的鞭毛运动，有助于理解活性物质的自组织动力学。
