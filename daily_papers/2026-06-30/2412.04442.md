# Linear-Scaling Potential-Free Data-Driven Molecular Dynamics for Arbitrary-Sized Water Clusters $(\text{H}_2\text{O})_n$

**arXiv**: [2412.04442](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.04442)
**Authors**: Hongyu Yan, Yong Wei, Minghan Chen, Hanning Chen
**Categories**: physics.chem-ph

## Abstract

arXiv:2412.04442v4 Announce Type: replace-cross 
Abstract: Conventional molecular dynamics (MD) simulation approaches, such as $\textit{ab initio}$ MD (AIMD) and empirical force field MD (EFFMD), face significant trade-offs between physical accuracy and computational efficiency. This work presents a linear-scaling potential-free data-driven molecular dynamics (PDMD) framework for predicting system energy and atomic forces of arbitrary-sized water clusters $(\text{H}_2\text{O})_n$. Specifically, PDMD employs a Gaussian-based atomic geometry descriptor to generate high-dimensional, equivariant features, then leverages ChemGNN, a graph neural network model that adaptively learns the atomic chemical environments without requiring $\textit{a priori}$ knowledge. Through an iterative self-consistent training approach, the converged PDMD achieves a mean absolute error of 1.39 meV/atom for energy, outperforming other state-of-the-art models such as DeepMD, MACE, NequIP, and SevenNet by at least 2.6x in accuracy with the same dataset. As a result, the linear-scaling PDMD can reproduce the AIMD properties of water clusters at orders-of-magnitude lower computational cost, as illustrated by simulations of systems consisting of thousands or more molecules. These results demonstrate that the proposed PDMD offers multiphase predictive power and enables ultra-fast, general-purpose MD simulations while retaining AIMD-level accuracy. This accuracy is achieved by efficiently capturing many-body potentials that are critical in numerous polyatomic systems but are often missing in EFFMD. Moreover, we have constructed an $\textit{ab initio}$ dataset with over 300,000 $(\text{H}_2\text{O})_n$ structures, standardized in a unified PyTorch Geometric framework, to support scalable evaluation of artificial intelligence methods for molecular dynamics.

# Linear-Scaling Potential-Free Data-Driven Molecular Dynamics for Arbitrary-Sized Water Clusters $(\text{H}_2\text{O})_n$

> **ArXiv**: [2412.04442](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.04442) | **Authors**: Hongyu Yan, Yong Wei, Minghan Chen, Hanning Chen

# 论文阅读笔记

## 一句话总结
本文提出了一种线性标度的无势函数数据驱动分子动力学（PDMD）框架，用于任意大小水簇的系统能量和原子力预测，在保持从头算分子动力学（AIMD）精度的同时，实现了数量级更低的计算成本。

## 研究问题
传统分子动力学模拟方法（如AIMD和经验力场MD）在物理精度和计算效率之间存在显著权衡，如何实现既高效又精确的分子动力学模拟是核心问题。

## 核心方法
- 采用基于高斯的原子几何描述子生成高维等变特征。
- 利用图神经网络模型ChemGNN自适应学习原子化学环境，无需先验知识。
- 通过迭代自洽训练方式，实现线性标度的势函数自由数据驱动分子动力学（PDMD）。

## 主要结果
- PDMD在能量预测上达到平均绝对误差1.39 meV/atom，相比DeepMD、MACE、NequIP和SevenNet等模型，精度提升至少2.6倍。
- 能够以线性复杂度重现AIMD的水簇性质，模拟数千及以上分子系统时计算成本大幅降低。
- 构建了包含超过30万个(H₂O)ₙ结构的从头算数据集，并标准化为统一的PyTorch Geometric框架。

## 潜在价值
该工作为大规模、高精度、通用目的分子动力学模拟提供了有效方案，尤其适用于需要捕捉多体势的多元体系，有助于推动数据驱动方法在计算化学和材料科学领域的实际应用。
